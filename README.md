# Bihar Special Intensive Review (SIR) 2025: Data on Objections

In June 24, 2025, the Election Commission of India (ECI) [announced a Special Intensive Survey (SIR) in Bihar](https://www.scobserver.in/wp-content/uploads/2025/07/Bihar-SIR-Notification.pdf), just 4 months preceding the upcoming state assemble elections.
This sudden decision also included not considering the Aadhar Card as valid identity proof (this particular provision was later [reversed by the Supreme Court of India](https://www.eci.gov.in/eci-backend/public/api/download?url=LMAhAK6sOPBp%252FNFF0iRfXbEB1EVSLT41NNLRjYNJJP1KivrUxbfqkDatmHy12e%252FzIC5IR1A3V88Anuk8RlyPGUF%252BbHgDtAAaPIybju7jiaurtAWxary%252FTnv9pkg%252B7L5HMYDIDJHJqInT%252FcySBQlaAA%253D%253D)).

On August 21, 2025 the [ECI published its draft voter rolls](https://www.thehindu.com/news/national/sir-row-supreme-court-orders-eci-to-publish-list-of-names-excluded-from-bihar-draft-roll/article69932462.ece), which excluded about 65 lakh names of past voters.
Voters were provided an opportunity to file objections if they believed their names were wrongfully removed.

The data from these objection forms were later made public.

This project scrapes the data from about 16,000 pages of PDFs provided by the ECI, which includes details of about 900,000 objectors.
The repository includes all code, files, and data involved in the process.
The repaired version of the data can be found [here](https://github.com/JaPrad/bihar_draft_sir_2025_objections/blob/main/data/addition_csv/intermediate/repair_stage1.csv).

Future plans include analysing this data to check for patterns of exclusion among women and marginalized communities from this data.
